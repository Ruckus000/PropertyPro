# SECTION 5: THE "COLLECTED YESES" INVENTORY â€” Both Channels

> **What this section is for:** Every turnaround in Section 3 has a Minimize step. The Minimize step only works if you have ammunition â€” specific things the prospect said, agreed to, or acknowledged earlier in the process. This section tells you exactly what to capture, when to capture it, how to solicit it, and how to deploy it when it matters. If you skip this discipline, your Minimize step becomes generic hand-waving ("You seemed interested before...") instead of a precise, undeniable summary of their own words.

> **The psychology:** People have a deep need to be consistent with things they've already said and done. When you reflect a prospect's own statements back to them during a negotiation, you create cognitive dissonance between their objection and their prior agreements. They can't easily say "it's too expensive" after they've already told you they spend 15 hours a month on manual work, agreed that $3/unit is reasonable, and said the demo was "exactly what we need." The objection doesn't disappear â€” but it loses its weight. That's what collected yeses do.

## A. BOARD CHANNEL "YESES" TRACKER

### Stage 1: Cold Contact â†’ Meeting Scheduled

These are the earliest yeses. They're thin â€” the prospect barely knows you â€” but they matter because they establish the compliance problem as real and the conversation as worth having.

**YES #1: Compliance Awareness Acknowledgment**

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What to capture** | Whether the board president knows about the expanded 25-unit threshold and acknowledges their association falls under it |

## C. THE "YESES" CAPTURE SYSTEM

> **The constraint:** You're a solo founder making 5â€“10 calls a day. You don't have a CRM. The capture system must take less than 5 minutes per conversation or you won't use it.

### Recommended Format: Spreadsheet + Voice Memos

**Primary tool:** A single Google Sheet with one row per prospect, structured as below.

**Backup tool:** Your phone's voice memo app. After every call, before you do anything else, record a 60-second voice memo with the prospect's name and the 3â€“5 most important things they said. ### When to Write Things Down

**During the call:** Only write down numbers and names. Don't try to capture full quotes while listening â€” you'll miss the next thing they say. Jot their time estimate, the feature they reacted to, and any name they mention (a board member, a PM company, a community).

**Immediately after the call (within 5 minutes):** Record the voice memo. Then open the spreadsheet and fill in the verbatim quotes while they're fresh. This is the most important 5 minutes in your sales process. The exact phrasing fades fast.

**End of day:** Review all new entries. Highlight the "Strongest Overall Yes" for each prospect. This is the quote you'll reach for first during any future turnaround.
