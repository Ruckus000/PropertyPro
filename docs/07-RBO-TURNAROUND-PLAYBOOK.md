## The Pitch Foundation: What You're Actually Selling

Before any turnaround works, you need to internalize what you're selling. It's not compliance software. It's not a website. Here's the framing that should underpin every conversation:

**Lead with operational pain.** Every self-managed condo board is running their community through a cobbled-together mess of personal email inboxes, paper flyers in the lobby, phone calls to the president's cell at 9 PM, filing cabinets full of documents nobody can find, and a treasurer's personal Excel spreadsheet. That's the real problem. It exists today, independent of any statute.

**Position compliance as the entry point, not the value proposition.** The state now requires you to have a website â€” so you have to do *something*. The question is whether you do the bare minimum and get a static compliance page, or whether you use this as the forcing function to finally get your community's operations out of email chains and filing cabinets.

**Use enforcement as supporting evidence, not the headline.** Enforcement risk is real (owner-initiated records requests, $50/day damages), but it's a secondary motivator. If you lead with fear, you attract customers who stop paying the moment they stop feeling afraid. If you lead with operational value, you attract customers who stay because the product makes their lives easier.

**The one-sentence pitch:** "The state says you need a compliant website â€” we give you that, plus the operational tools that replace the email-and-spreadsheet chaos your board is currently running on."

## The Blount Prospecting Turnaround Framework

| Step | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **LEDGE** | A short, empathetic statement that acknowledges their response without agreeing with it. Buys you 2â€“3 seconds of emotional processing time and signals you're not about to steamroll them. |
| **DISRUPT** | A brief, unexpected reframe that breaks the pattern they expect. They expect you to argue, pitch harder, or give up. Instead, you offer a perspective that makes their current position feel incomplete. Pulls them toward you instead of pushing. |
| **ASK** | A specific, low-commitment, assumptive ask for a next step. NOT the sale. Typically 10â€“15 minutes of their time. Use either/or time frames to convert a yes/no decision into a scheduling decision. |

### RBO Taxonomy

| Type | What It Sounds Like | What's Really Happening |
|------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Reflex Response** | "Send me info," "I'm busy," "Not interested" | Autopilot. They haven't processed your message. Not a real "no." |

| **True Objection** | "Too expensive," "No enforcement," "We'll DIY it" | Genuine concern or belief. Requires substantive engagement and reframing. |
