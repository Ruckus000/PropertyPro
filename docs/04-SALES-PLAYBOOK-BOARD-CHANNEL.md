### What Changed and Why

**Discovery + demo merged into one 30-minute meeting.** Three separate scheduled interactions before anything happened was too much friction. Every calendar coordination costs 3â€“7 days. The combined meeting starts with 10 minutes of discovery, transitions into a live demo of *their own pre-built portal*, and ends with the audit offer.

**Portal pre-built before the meeting, not after.** Instead of demoing the fictional "Palm Gardens" and then spending 48 hours building their portal, you now pull their declaration and bylaws from county records *before the meeting* and demo their actual community. This eliminates the imagination gap and removes the post-demo setup delay.

**Audit compressed from 30 days to 14 days.** Meaningful engagement happens in weeks 1â€“2. Weeks 3â€“4 of the old model added resident adoption, which is nice but doubled the timeline. A 14-day audit with a hard review on day 12 creates the same loss-aversion dynamics at half the cycle length. For a solo founder capped at 3â€“5 concurrent audits, this means nearly double the throughput per quarter.

**Mutual action plan added for board-vote deals.** A one-page shared document co-created with the champion that maps out the compliance problem, audit results, board meeting date, vote item, and contract. Research shows deals using mutual action plans see ~13% higher win rates and significantly fewer stalls from "we didn't get to it at the meeting."

**Self-service path added for compliance-basic tier.** The $99/mo compliance-only tier is sub-$200 MRR. At that price point, human-touch sales is inefficient. A self-service signup lets panicking board presidents solve their problem at 11 PM without waiting for a Thursday call. This frees your bandwidth for the $199+ and PM deals where personal selling moves the needle.

**Hard constraint remains:** Maximum 3â€“5 concurrent audits. Qualify ruthlessly before offering one.
